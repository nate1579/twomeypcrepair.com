Common Mac Issues We Fix

Cracked Screens

  • Visible cracks or lines on display
  • Dead pixels or discolored areas
  • Flickering or dim screen
  • External monitor works fine

Battery Problems

  • Won't hold charge
  • Rapid battery drain
  • Swollen battery (trackpad lifting)
  • "Service Battery" warning

Software Glitches

  • Frequent crashes or freezing
  • Slow startup or shutdown
  • Applications won't open
  • Strange error messages

Overheating

  • Fan running constantly
  • Very hot to touch
  • Unexpected shutdowns
  • Performance throttling

Preventive Maintenance Tips

Keep It Clean

Regular cleaning of vents and fans prevents overheating. Use compressed air monthly to blow out dust.

Manage Storage

Keep at least 15% of your drive free for optimal performance. Use Storage Management tools in macOS.

Update Regularly

Install macOS updates and security patches promptly. Keep applications updated for best performance.

Battery Care

Avoid extreme temperatures and deep discharges. Use optimized battery charging when available.

Backup Data

Use Time Machine for regular backups. Consider additional cloud storage for important files.

Gentle Handling

Close your MacBook carefully and avoid putting pressure on the screen. Use a quality case for transport.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does Mac repair take (1-3 days)?

Most Mac repairs are completed within 1-3 days depending on the complexity and parts availability. Simple repairs like battery replacement may be done same-day, while logic board repairs or screen replacements may take 2-3 days.

Is it worth repairing an old Mac?

It depends on the model age and repair cost. Macs generally have excellent longevity, and repairs are often cost-effective compared to replacement. We'll provide honest advice based on your specific situation and budget.

Do I need an appointment?

Walk-ins are welcome, but scheduling an appointment ensures we can dedicate time to properly diagnose your Mac. Appointments also help reduce your wait time and guarantee service availability.

What if my Mac won't turn on?

Bring it in for diagnosis. Many "dead" Macs can be repaired - it could be a power supply, logic board, or software issue. Our diagnostic service will determine if your Mac can be saved and at what cost.
